Make Caste Certificate Online in Assam

A caste certificate is an official document that certifies that an individual belongs to a particular caste, especially among the Scheduled Castes (SC), Scheduled Tribes (ST), or Other Backward Classes (OBC) communities. In Assam, the caste certificate is essential for availing various government schemes, reservation benefits, educational admissions, and other welfare-related opportunities. The Government of Assam has made the process simple and accessible by enabling online applications through the official portals.

In this article, we at Skill2Job aim to inform and guide users about the correct and lawful method to apply for a caste certificate online without depending on agents or middlemen who often charge unreasonably high fees for simple online procedures. Many individuals face unnecessary financial burden because of such practices at local desks or shops that exploit a lack of awareness. Our purpose is to empower citizens with accurate information so they can apply for government documents confidently, free of extra costs or unfair assistance.

Eligibility for Caste Certificate in Assam

To obtain a caste certificate in Assam, applicants must meet certain eligibility requirements set by the government. These criteria ensure that only individuals who genuinely belong to the declared caste category can access the benefits associated with caste certification. The certificate plays an important role in availing government schemes, educational reservations, and employment opportunities. Below are the specific eligibility requirements.

  • Any permanent resident of Assam belonging to SC, ST (Plain), ST (Hills), or OBC/MOBC categories.
  • The applicant must have valid proof of residence and caste.
  • Supporting documents must show that the family has historically been recognized under the same caste category.
  • The certificate is generally issued by the Deputy Commissioner, Sub-Divisional Officer, or Circle Officer of the respective district.

How to Apply for Caste Certificate Online in Assam

There are two main methods to apply for a caste certificate online in Assam ‚Äî through the Sewa Setu Portal and the e-District Assam Portal. Among these, the Sewa Setu portal is the most convenient and recommended option for the general public. The e-District Assam portal is primarily intended for CSC (Common Service Centre) agents and requires login credentials that are difficult for ordinary citizens to obtain without prior approval from the Deputy Commissioner‚Äôs office.

Method 1: Apply Through Sewa Setu Portal (Recommended)

  • Visit the official Sewa Setu portal at https://sewasetu.assam.gov.in.
  • On the homepage, navigate to the ‚ÄúApply for Caste Certificate‚Äù section.
  • Fill out the online form with accurate personal details, address, and caste category.
  • Upload all required documents, including proof of identity, residence, and caste verification documents.
  • Review the form carefully to ensure there are no mistakes.
  • Submit the application and download the acknowledgment slip or reference number for tracking.

The Sewa Setu portal is designed to be user-friendly and does not require prior registration or approval from district offices. Applicants can directly apply and track their application status using the reference number provided after submission. This method is efficient, cost-effective, and best suited for the general public.

Method 2: Apply Through e-District Assam Portal

  • Visit the official e-District Assam portal at https://edistrict.assam.gov.in.
  • Log in with your e-District credentials. If you do not have them, you must apply at your nearest Deputy Commissioner‚Äôs office.
  • After logging in, search for ‚ÄúCaste Certificate‚Äù under available services and click ‚ÄúApply.‚Äù
  • Fill in all necessary personal and caste-related details carefully.
  • Upload scanned copies of all required documents.
  • Submit your application and download the acknowledgment slip containing your reference number.

While this method is officially available, it is less preferred for individual applicants because obtaining an e-District ID and password is not straightforward. It is mainly used by authorised CSC operators to apply on behalf of others.

  • Visit the same portal where you applied (either Sewa Setu or e-District Assam).
  • Click on ‚ÄúTrack Application Status.‚Äù
  • Enter your reference number or application ID and select your district.
  • Click ‚ÄúSubmit‚Äù to view the current status of your application.

The system will display whether your application is under process, approved, rejected, or marked as revert back for correction. Once your application is approved, you can download the caste certificate directly from the same portal.

What After Successful Submission?

After successful submission of the online application through either the Sewa Setu or e-District Assam portal, applicants must wait for verification and approval by the concerned authorities. You can regularly monitor the status using your reference number, as both portals are linked and provide real-time updates on the progress.

If your application shows the status ‚ÄúRevert Back,‚Äù it indicates that there is an issue with your submission, such as unclear or missing documents. Once the revised application is submitted, it will again be reviewed by the verification officer. If no revert back is shown, the application will continue processing. Generally, the caste certificate takes around three to four months to be approved, depending on the verification and workload of the respective office.

Upon approval, you may receive an SMS notification on your registered mobile number. However, if no message is received, applicants can manually check the status online. The caste certificate can then be downloaded in PDF format from the same portal once it is marked as approved.
