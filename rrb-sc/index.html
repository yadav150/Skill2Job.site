RRB Section Controller Recruitment 2025

The Railway Recruitment Boards (RRB), under the Ministry of Railways, Government of India, has released Centralised Employment Notice (CEN) No. 04/2025 for the recruitment of Section Controller posts. This drive seeks to fill 368 vacancies across various railway zones in India.

Eligible candidates who wish to apply can do so through the official RRB websites. The online application process starts on 15 September 2025, and the last date to submit applications is 14 October 2025.

RRB Section Controller Recruitment Details

Post Name: Section Controller

Total Vacancies: 368

Organization: Railway Recruitment Board (RRB), Ministry of Railways

Salary/Pay Scale: The Section Controller position is classified under Pay Level 6 of the 7th CPC Pay Matrix, with a starting salary of ‚Çπ35,400 per month, along with other allowances as applicable.

Educational Qualification: Applicants must hold a Graduate Degree from a recognized university or its equivalent. Candidates awaiting their final results are not eligible to apply.

Age Limit: Candidates must be between 18 and 33 years old as of 1st January 2025.

Age Relaxation: Upper age limit relaxation is provided as follows ‚Äì 3 years for OBC candidates, 5 years for SC/ST candidates, and 10 years for PwBD candidates.

RRB Examination Fee Details

Examination Fee: Candidates belonging to PwBD, Female, Transgender, Ex-Servicemen, SC, ST, Minority Communities, and Economically Weaker Sections are required to pay an examination fee of ‚Çπ250/-. All other applicants must pay ‚Çπ500/-.

Refund Policy: Eligible candidates from PwBD, Female, Transgender, Ex-Servicemen, SC, ST, Minority Communities, and Economically Weaker Sections will receive a full refund of ‚Çπ250/- after the examination. Candidates who paid ‚Çπ500/- will be refunded ‚Çπ400/- post-examination.

Payment Mode: The examination fee can be paid online through Credit/Debit Cards, Net Banking, or UPI. Payments through any other method will not be accepted.

RRB Selection Process

The recruitment for the Section Controller post will be carried out through multiple phases to assess candidates' eligibility and suitability for the role.

Phase 1: Single-Phase Computer Based Test (CBT): This test will consist of 100 objective-type questions to be completed within 120 minutes. Each incorrect answer will attract a negative marking of one-third.

Phase 2: Computer Based Aptitude Test (CBAT): Based on their CBT scores, candidates up to eight times the total number of vacancies will be shortlisted for the CBAT. This test will not have any negative marking.

Phase 3: Document Verification (DV): Candidates will be called for document verification according to the combined merit of CBT (70% weightage) and CBAT (30% weightage).

Phase 4: Medical Examination: Candidates who successfully clear the document verification phase will undergo a medical examination to ensure they meet the A-2 medical standards.

How to Apply for RRB Section Controller Recruitment?

Eligible candidates interested in the RRB Section Controller recruitment can submit their applications online by following the outlined procedure. It is strongly advised to read the official notification carefully before applying. Remember, each candidate is allowed to apply for only one RRB; submitting multiple applications may lead to disqualification.

To begin, visit the official website of the Railway Recruitment Board. New applicants must create an account, while existing users can log in with their credentials. Complete the application form with correct personal, educational, and contact information. You will also need to upload a recent live photograph taken via webcam and a scanned copy of your signature in the specified format.

After entering all the details and uploading the required documents, proceed to the online payment portal to pay the application fee using a debit card, credit card, net banking, or UPI. Once the payment is successfully completed, review your application thoroughly and submit it. It is recommended to download or print a copy of the submitted application for your records.
