SSC CPO Selection Process 2025

The recruitment for Sub-Inspector posts involves a multi-stage selection procedure. Initially, candidates must appear for the Computer Based Examination (Paper-I). Those who qualify will undergo the Physical Standard Test (PST) and Physical Endurance Test (PET).

Candidates who successfully clear the PST and PET will then take the Computer Based Examination (Paper-II), followed by a Detailed Medical Examination (DME). The final selection is determined based on the combined performance in Paper-I and Paper-II, provided all other stages are successfully completed.

SSC CPO Examination Scheme 2025

Paper-I: Computer Based Examination

Paper-I evaluates candidates across four key sections. Each section contains 50 questions, with a maximum of 50 marks per section. The total duration of the examination is 2 hours. A negative marking of 0.25 marks is applied for each incorrect answer.

Paper-II consists of English Language and Comprehension questions. The exam carries 200 questions for a total of 200 marks. Negative marking of 0.25 marks per wrong answer applies here as well.
