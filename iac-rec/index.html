IAC Eligibility Criteria

To apply for these posts, candidates must fulfill specific educational and age requirements.

Educational Qualification:
Vehicle Mechanic, Electrician, Telecom Mechanic, Engineer Equipment Mechanic: 10+2 pass with a certificate from a recognized ITI in the relevant trade, OR candidates who are Armed Forces personnel/ex-servicemen from the appropriate trade.
Machinist, Fitter, Welder, Tin and Copper Smith, Upholster: ITI certificate from a recognized institute in the respective trade.
Storekeeper / Lower Division Clerk (LDC): 12th pass. LDC candidates must also have a typing speed of 35 wpm in English or 30 wpm in Hindi on a computer.
Fireman: Matriculation (10th pass), physically fit, and knowledgeable about fire-fighting equipment.
Cook: Matriculation (10th pass) with knowledge of Indian cooking.
Tradesman Mate / Washerman: Matriculation (10th pass) from a recognized board.

Age Limit: Candidates must be between 18 and 25 years of age.
Age Relaxation:
SC/ST: 5 years
OBC (Non-creamy layer): 3 years
Persons with Benchmark Disability (PwBD): 10 years (13 years for OBC, 15 years for SC/ST)

IAC Salary Structure

Securing a position through this recruitment comes with an attractive salary along with several additional benefits. The salary is determined according to the 7th Central Pay Commission (CPC), ensuring fair compensation based on the post and pay level.

The Pay Level determines the basic pay. For instance, a post at Pay Level 2 has a basic pay ranging from Rs. 19,900 to Rs. 63,200. In addition to the basic pay, candidates receive various allowances that increase the overall monthly income.

Dearness Allowance (DA): Provided to compensate for inflation and revised twice annually.
House Rent Allowance (HRA): Helps cover rental expenses and varies depending on the city of posting.
Transport Allowance (TA): Supports daily commuting costs between home and workplace.
Other Perks: Additional benefits include contributions to the National Pension System (NPS), comprehensive medical facilities for employees and their families, and paid leaves, making these jobs financially secure and highly desirable.

IAC Selection Process

The selection process aims to identify the most suitable candidates for each post and consists of multiple stages.

Tier 1: Computer-Based Test (OMR Based)
This is an offline objective-type written exam of 150 marks, with a duration of 2 hours. There is a negative marking of 0.25 for each incorrect answer. The exam pattern differs slightly based on the post:

Technical Posts (Fireman, Electrician, etc.): General Intelligence & Reasoning ‚Äì 25 Qs (25 Marks), General Awareness ‚Äì 25 Qs (25 Marks), General English ‚Äì 25 Qs (25 Marks), Numerical Aptitude ‚Äì 25 Qs (25 Marks), Trade-Specific ‚Äì 50 Qs (50 Marks).
LDC, Storekeeper: General Intelligence & Reasoning ‚Äì 25 Qs (25 Marks), General Awareness ‚Äì 25 Qs (25 Marks), General English ‚Äì 50 Qs (50 Marks), Numerical Aptitude ‚Äì 50 Qs (50 Marks).
Tradesman Mate, Cook: General Intelligence & Reasoning ‚Äì 50 Qs (50 Marks), General Awareness ‚Äì 50 Qs (50 Marks), General English ‚Äì 25 Qs (25 Marks), Numerical Aptitude ‚Äì 25 Qs (25 Marks).

Tier 2: Skill / Physical Test
Candidates who qualify the written test are called for a skill or physical test depending on the post. This stage is qualifying in nature, and marks obtained are not added to the final merit list:

LDC: Typing test to assess the required speed.
Fireman: Physical fitness and endurance test including height, weight, chest measurements, carrying a person, long jump, and rope climbing.
Other Trades: Practical skill test to evaluate proficiency in the specific trade.
